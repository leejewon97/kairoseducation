Study Pathways

Three ways to get to Korea.

Whether you're starting from zero Korean or aiming for a full scholarship, there's a clear path. Kairos helps you identify which one fits — and executes it.

A
Language Program
Study at a Korean Language Center
D-4-1 Visa · Language Training

Enroll in a university language center for 3 months to 2 years of immersive Korean. No prior Korean required. The most accessible entry point — and the natural next step for anyone who's been learning Korean online.

  • Yonsei KLI · Korea U KLC · SNU LEI · Sogang · Ewha
  • 4 intakes per year: March · June · September · December
  • ~$1,200–1,800 USD per term (3 months)
  • Requires ~$10,000 USD proof of funds + Certificate of Admission
  • Embassy application in your home country (15 business days)
B
Degree Program
Undergraduate or Graduate Degree
D-2 Visa · Student Visa

Earn a full BA, MA, or PhD from a Korean university. Many programs are available in English (no TOPIK required). Korean-taught programs require TOPIK 3–4. Korea's SKY universities are globally recognized.

  • SNU · Korea University · Yonsei · KAIST · POSTECH · Ewha
  • 4-year BA · 2-year MA · 3-year PhD
  • TOPIK 3–4 for Korean-taught · English programs available
  • SOP · transcripts · recommendation letters required
  • Intakes: Spring (March) and Fall (September)
C
GKS Scholarship
Fully Funded by the Korean Government
GKS / KGSP · NIIED Program

The Global Korea Scholarship covers full tuition + ~$900/month stipend + airfare + health insurance. One of the most generous government scholarships in the world. Highly competitive — this is where Kairos adds the most value.

  • Covers: Tuition · Monthly stipend · Airfare · Insurance
  • Undergrad: 1yr Korean + 4yr degree · Grad: 1yr Korean + 2–3yr MA/PhD
  • Embassy Track (via Korean Embassy) or University Track
  • 2,000+ graduate slots · 280 undergraduate slots globally in 2026
  • Russia, Ukraine, CIS, EU, SE Asia, Africa — all eligible countries

FAQ

Common questions.

The Global Korea Scholarship (GKS), also called KGSP, is a fully-funded Korean government scholarship administered by NIIED. It covers tuition, a monthly stipend (~$900 USD), airfare, and health insurance. In 2026, approximately 2,000 graduate slots and 280 undergraduate slots were available globally. It's competitive — but with the right application strategy, strong candidates from eligible countries (which includes Russia, Ukraine, most EU nations, and over 100 others) have a real shot. This is exactly where the Kairos Summit package is designed to help.
Yes — Russia is an eligible country for Korean student visas and the GKS scholarship. Korean embassies in Moscow, Vladivostok, St. Petersburg, and Irkutsk process D-4 and D-2 visa applications. Processing typically takes around 15 business days. Some students face challenges with international wire transfers to Korean institutions due to banking restrictions — Kairos helps you navigate these logistics as part of any package.
TOPIK (Test of Proficiency in Korean) is the official Korean language proficiency test, with levels 1–6. For language center programs (D-4 visa), you do not need TOPIK. For degree programs taught in Korean, most universities require TOPIK Level 3 or 4. For English-taught programs, TOPIK is not required, but having it strengthens your application. The GKS scholarship does not require TOPIK at application — you complete one year of Korean language training after acceptance.
Language center programs cost approximately $1,200–$1,800 USD per term (3 months), plus living expenses (~$700–1,200/month in Seoul depending on housing). Degree program tuition ranges from $3,000–$8,000 USD per year at most Korean universities — significantly cheaper than US or UK equivalents. If you receive the GKS scholarship, tuition and a monthly stipend (~$900 USD) are fully covered by the Korean government.
