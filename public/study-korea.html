Study Pathways

Three ways into Korea.

Whether you are starting from zero Korean or aiming for a full scholarship, there is a clear route. Ron helps you find the one that fits, then walks you through it.

A
Language Program
D-4-1 visa · Study at a Korean language center
Three months to two years of immersive Korean, starting from the alphabet. The most accessible way in.
  • Yonsei KLI, Korea U KLC, SNU LEI, Sogang, Ewha
  • Four intakes a year: March, June, September, December
  • About $1,200 to $1,800 per term
  • Roughly $10,000 in proof of funds required
  • Applied for at your home country's Korean embassy
B
Degree Program
D-2 visa · Undergraduate or graduate degree
A full bachelor's, master's, or PhD. Many programs are taught in English with no TOPIK required.
  • SNU, Korea University, Yonsei, KAIST, POSTECH, Ewha
  • 4-year BA · 2-year MA · 3-year PhD
  • TOPIK 3 to 4 for Korean-taught programs
  • Statement of purpose, transcripts, recommendations
  • Intakes in spring (March) and fall (September)
C
GKS Scholarship
Fully funded by the Korean government
Full tuition, a monthly stipend of about $900, airfare, and insurance. Highly competitive, where Ron adds the most value.
  • Covers tuition, stipend, airfare, and insurance
  • Undergrad: 1 year Korean plus a 4-year degree
  • Grad: 1 year Korean plus a 2 to 3 year MA or PhD
  • Apply through the embassy or university track
  • 2,000+ grad and 280+ undergrad places in 2026

FAQ

Common questions.

No. Language center programs (D-4-1 visa) are built for complete beginners, starting from the alphabet. Yonsei KLI, Korea University KLC, SNU LEI, and Sogang all offer Level 1 courses that assume no prior knowledge. If you have already studied, you will test into a higher level.

The Global Korea Scholarship (GKS, also called KGSP) is a fully funded government scholarship run by NIIED. It covers tuition, a monthly stipend of about $900, airfare, and health insurance. In 2026 there were roughly 2,000 graduate and 280 undergraduate places worldwide. It is competitive, but with the right strategy, strong candidates from over 100 countries have a real shot. This is exactly what the Summit package is built for.

Korea welcomes students from over 100 countries for both the D-4 language visa and the D-2 student visa, and Korean consulates operate worldwide. Visa processing usually takes one to three weeks depending on where you are. Ron helps you find the right consulate, prepare your documents, and handle any country-specific steps.

TOPIK is the official Korean proficiency test, levels 1 to 6. For language center programs you do not need it. For degree programs taught in Korean, most universities want TOPIK Level 3 or 4. For English-taught programs it is not required, though it strengthens your application. GKS does not require TOPIK at the application stage; you complete a year of Korean after acceptance.

Language programs run about $1,200 to $1,800 per term, plus living costs of roughly $700 to $1,200 a month in Seoul. Degree tuition is about $3,000 to $8,000 a year, well below US or UK equivalents. With the GKS scholarship, tuition and a monthly stipend are fully covered.
